The Game

The earliest origins of Chess pinpoints to India at around 7th century. Chatarunga as it was called, is the precursor of chess. The name Chatarunga translates to four division: infantry, cavalry, elephantry and chariotry. These pieces would later evolve to become the pawn, knight, bishop and rook. The game name went through many transformations as it was spread to different countries, Persia called it Shatranj Mongols called it Shatar, ultimately in Europe it finally became Chess.

In earlier accounts of the game. Chess was played with a dice however, the dice was later removed due to gambling restrictions. Some fun facts about the original oldest version. The bishop was only able to move two squares, sideways and front-and-back and could jump over a piece in-between, the queen was able to move only one square diagonally,the pawn could only move one square forward and take over another piece diagonally and be promoted to a Queen.
